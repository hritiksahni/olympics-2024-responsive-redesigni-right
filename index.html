    OLYMPIC GAMES

    PARIS

    The Paris 2024 Olympic and Paralympic Games will be the biggest event ever organised in France. The Olympic games will take place from 26 July to 11 August 2024. The spectacle taking place during those weeks will go down in history and Paris will be the centre of the world – the world of sport and so much more. The Games are a popular, multicultural festival, a celebration to share with the rest of the world. They represent a new adventure that will embark France on an experience unlike anything it has seen before.

    the vision of paris 2024

    Paris 2024 will see a new vision of Olympism in action, delivered in a unique spirit of international celebration.


    We will offer one of the world’s most inspirational cities as a memorable stage for the athletes – and a truly global platform to promote them, and their incredible stories.


    And we will partner with the entire Olympic family to demonstrate that, more than ever after an extremely challenging period, sport has a unique power to help create a better world.


    Our plan features 95 percent existing or temporary venues, and every single one has a clear, defined legacy aligned with the city’s long-term development plans.


    The sporting celebration will flow along the Seine, from the new Olympic Village, just 15 minutes from Paris city centre, to such city centre landmarks as the Eiffel Tower and the Grand Palais.

    WE'VE GOT ANSWERS

    How many spectators will be able to attend the ceremony?

    We have chosen to organise the opening ceremony for the Paris 2024 Olympic Games at the heart of the City so that we can share this unique experience with even more people. At least 600,000 spectators will be able to be part of the celebrations, along the 6 km route, on the lower and upper quays of the River Seine. This is around 10 times the capacity of an Olympic stadium.

    How long is the parade route for the opening ceremony?

    The procession along the banks of the River Seine will run for 6 km, between Pont d’Austerlitz and Pont d’Iéna. The parade will come to the end of its route in front of Trocadéro, for a spectacular finale.

    Will the ceremony be accessible for everyone, including people with disabilities?

    With its commitments to promoting inclusion and openness, Paris 2024 wants to open up access to this ceremony as widely as possible. It therefore incorporates the principles of universal accessibility into its infrastructures (stands), while taking into consideration the topographical constraints that we will have to work with.

    How can you attend the opening ceremony of the Paris 2024 Olympic Games?

    Whether you live in Paris, Saint Denis, Greater Paris, the rest of France or somewhere else in the world, everyone is invited to the party! Paid seating will be located on the lower banks of the River Seine as well as on certain bridges and other nearby areas. Spectators will enjoy an unforgettable collective experience, up close to the performances and the athletes travelling down the river. These spots will make up the very decor of this opening ceremony, combining history and architecture and giving spectators the chance to be part of a unique moment.

    When are the Paris 2024 Olympic and Paralympic Games?


    The Paris 2024 Olympic Games will take place from 26 July to 11 August.

    The Paris 2024 Paralympic Games will take place from 28 August to 8 September.

    How many tickets will be available for the Olympic and Paralympic Games?


    10 million tickets will go on sale for the Olympic Games.

    Almost 3.4 million tickets will go on sale for the Paralympic Games.

    How much will tickets to the Olympic & Paralympic Games cost?


    Prices for all Olympic sports will start at €24.

    Prices for all Paralympic sports will start at €15.

    Is it possible to suggest educational resources to Paris 2024?


    You can share your experiences and propose educational tools and/or content by writing to: generation2024@paris2024.org.

    Where can I find the educational resources for Generation 2024?


    The educational resources are available to everyone on the Generation 2024 platform. They are aimed at everyone who would like to incorporate more sport into everyday life for young people, and particularly the educational community, parents of students and educators, as well as the students themselves.

    How can we show the film “Race”?


    Paris 2024 provides teachers with access to the film “Race”. To obtain and show this film to young people in your school, you will need to submit a request using the contact form available here [link 1]. Learn more about the film “Race”.

    What is the I’mPossible programme?

    Paris 2024 supports the use of the Olympic Values Education Programmes (OVEP), developed by the IOC, and I’mPossible, developed by the International Paralympic Committee (IPC). The I’mPossible programme is a particularly valuable tool for school teachers. It helps set up practical applications, supports increased awareness and makes it possible to change perspectives on disabilities. Paris 2024 would like to support teachers with these educational programmes to ensure that they get the most out of them.

    How long does the Cultural Olympiad last?

    The Cultural Olympiad is taking place over the four years preceding the Paris 2024 Games and will end with the Paralympic Games, on 8 September 2024. A cultural programme will be presented each season in all the areas wishing to take part and shine a spotlight on sport and culture together.

    How are applicants assessed?

    Paris 2024 will define its assessment criteria for each volunteering opportunity, including interpersonal skills, availability and, in certain cases, expertise. The information provided by applicants in their application and, for certain duties, during additional assessments or interviews will enable us to identify future volunteers based on the criteria defined for each volunteering opportunity. No preparation is needed.

    Is there any help provided in order to fill in the application form?


    All candidates can call or e-mail the Paris 2024 volunteering relations centre for guidance, answers to their questions and assistance.

    Can we apply in groups?


    You must apply individually, but feel free to encourage your friends and family to apply too. Volunteers are assigned to teams, which gives them the opportunity to meet and get to know other fans of the Games.

    Do you need to live in France in order to be a volunteer?

    No, you do not need to live in France in order to be a volunteer. However, the events will take place in France, so it is up to each volunteer to make sure they have accommodation and the necessary authorisation to reside in France.
